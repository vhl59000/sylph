# Voice rules

Newsquick is a tool for professional traders. We speak operator-to-operator.

## Always

- Direct, declarative sentences. Lead with the punchline.
- Specific numbers over adjectives ($22B/mo, not "huge volume")
- Concrete examples (Domer, Théo, Evan Semet) over abstract personas
- "Newsquick" is one word, capitalized N only at sentence start or as a proper noun

## Never

Banned words and phrases:
- "leverage", "utilize", "synergize", "empower"
- "best-in-class", "cutting-edge", "next-generation"
- "seamless", "robust", "solution" (as a noun for the product)
- "AI trading bot" — we sell intelligence, not signals
- Comparisons to retail apps (Robinhood, Coinbase, etc.) — wrong reference class

## Comparable companies — in order

1. Newsquawk (FX news intelligence, $2-5K/mo) — primary comp
2. Bloomberg Terminal (equities, $24K/year) — anchor for the Bloomberg-moment narrative
3. Firstquack (Twitter-native breaking news) — adjacent

## Channel deltas

- LinkedIn: insights from building, never announcements
- Twitter/X: sharper, fragments OK, personality welcome
- Investor updates: numbers-led, no narrative without a metric attached
- Sales: lead with what changed in the trader's workflow, not feature lists
